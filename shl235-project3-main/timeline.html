Early Life (1955-1974)

1955

  • Steve Jobs was born on 1955 in San Francisco, California
  • He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs after Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali put him up for adoption

1961

  • Steve Jobs and his family move to Moutain View, a town that would later become a part of Silicon Valley
  • This geographical change to a town full of engineers naturally exposed Jobs to such field, shaping his interest early on in his life

1968

  • At the age of 13, Jobs meets Stephen Wozniak, 18 years old prodigy who later has an immense impact on Jobs' life

1972

  • After graduating from Homestead High School in Cupertino, Jobs enrolls in Reed College in Oregon
  • He drops out after a semester, focusing more on his general interests such as caligraphy and more
Mid-Life (1974-1992)

1976

  • Steve Jobs co-founds Apple Computer in his garage with his two close friends and coworkers, Wozniak and Ronald Wayne
  • They funded their new venture by selling Jobs' Volkswagen van and Wozniak's trasured scientific calculator

1977

  • Apple incorporates and releases the second generation of the Apple Computer, which becomes the first personal computer to be commercially available in color graphics and light plastic casing

1980

  • The net worth of Steve Jobs rises to upwards of $200 million after the company announces going to public
  • Jobs also begins a new project, LISA, named after his daughter who he denied paternity

1981

  • Jobs is removed from the LISA project due to his lack of leadership and short temper
  • He leads another project called Macintosh

1985

  • Although the intense three years he had spent in crafting the Macintosh,the slump that hit the PC industry overlooks its true success

1986

  • Despite the lack of success, Jobs's unbending attitude creates tension with the CEO, John Sculley, ultimately creating an indisputable feud that causes Jobs to resign
  • Creates NeXT with 5 other coworkers who also left Apple Inc.and reincorporates George Lucas's ILM into Pixar
Late Life (1993-2011)

1995

  • Jobs becomes the CEO and President of Pixar Animation Studios
  • After 9 years since incorporation as Pixar, the release of Toy Story sky rockets Jobs's net worth to upwards of $1.5 Billion

1996

  • Apple buys out NeXT for $400 million, eventually naming Jobs as 'informal advisor'

1997

  • After making his return to Apple Inc., becoming interim CEO after Gil Amelio's ousting
  • Marks the start of Think Different campaign, which defines the company for the next few years to come

1998

  • Introduction of the new iMac takes place in Cupertino

2000

  • Announces his new position as CEO of Apple at Macworld San Francisco, along with the revolutionary Mac OS X's Aqua interface

2002

  • Introduces iPod along with iTunes, unaware of the revolutionary change they will bring
  • Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, but is stern to receive any modern medical treatment for months

2005

  • The famous "Stay hungry, stay foolish" commencement speech is given at Stanford University
  • Disney buys Pixar for $7.4 Billion, placing Steve Jobs in the Disney board

2007

  • Introduces iPhone and MacBook Air, a new paradigm in the world technology with the use of touch screen and the thinnest portable computer respectively

2010

  • Jobs introduces iPad, Apple's first ever tablet
  • Jobs also take a leave of absence due to his worsening health condition without any end date

2011

  • Jobs holds his last ever Keynote in San Francisco, introducing a new offering: iCloud
  • Steve Jobs resigns from Apple
  • Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56 at his home with his family members beside him
